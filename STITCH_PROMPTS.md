# Stitch Design Prompts — Spyrosoft AI Hackathon Showcase

## Design System
Spyrosoft AI Hackathon — dark mode, primary `#4646CC` (deep purple), accent `#FF4D29` (red-orange). Fun, modern, techy vibe with subtle purple-to-orange gradients. Not corporate — more like a gaming/creative platform.

---

## Screen 1: Magic Link Login
Simple login page for hackathon showcase app. Dark background with subtle animated gradient (purple to orange). Center: card with slight glassmorphism effect. Inside card: "Spyrosoft AI Hackathon" logo/title at top with gradient text. Below: "Welcome, hacker!" heading. Short text: "Enter your email to access the showcase". Email input field with placeholder "your@email.com". Large "Send magic link" gradient button (purple to red-orange). Below button: small text "Check your inbox for the login link — no password needed." At the very bottom of the page: "Spyrosoft" small branding. Keep it minimal and inviting — one screen, one action.

## Screen 2: Magic Link Confirmation
Simple confirmation page after sending magic link. Same dark background with gradient. Center card: large envelope/mail icon with subtle animation (purple glow). Heading: "Check your email!" Subtext: "We sent a magic link to jan.kowalski@spyrosoft.com". Below: "Click the link in your email to log in. It expires in 15 minutes." Small link at bottom: "Didn't get it? Send again" and "Use a different email". Minimal, clean, reassuring.

## Screen 3: Landing Page (Project Grid)
Hackathon showcase landing page. Dark background. Top: "Spyrosoft AI Hackathon" logo/title with subtle gradient text (purple to orange). Below: grid of project cards (3-4 columns). Each card has: thumbnail image, project title, short description (2 lines max), team name/author, subtle category tag. Cards have slight glassmorphism effect with purple border glow on hover. At the bottom of the screen: large prominent CTA button "Watch All Demos" with gradient background (purple to red-orange) that leads to the TikTok-style video feed. Top-right: user avatar with dropdown. Navigation tabs: "Projects", "Vote", "My Project". Show ~8 sample project cards with placeholder content.

## Screen 4: Project Detail Modal
Modal overlay on dark background. Wide layout. Left side: landscape video player (16:9) with play button, progress bar, and "0:42 / 1:00" timestamp. Right side: project title (large), team members (avatars + names), and tabbed content below — Tab 1 "About": answers to predefined questions (What does it do?, How did you get the idea?, What was your journey?, Tech stack) displayed as labeled sections. Tab 2 "Presentation": embedded PDF viewer or download button. Close button top-right (X). Subtle gradient border around the modal.

## Screen 5: Video Feed (TikTok-style Browser)
Full-screen video browsing experience. Dark background. Center: large landscape video player taking up ~70% of screen width. Video plays automatically. Below video: project title, short description, team name — overlaid or just below the player. Navigation: up/down arrows on the sides OR scroll to switch between project videos. Top: "3 / 20" counter showing current position. Minimal UI — focus on the video. Small "View full project" link that opens the detail modal. Subtle autoplay animation/transition between videos.

## Screen 6: Voting Screen
Voting page. Dark background. Title: "Vote for your favorites" with gradient text. 3 category sections side by side (or stacked on mobile): "Best Overall", "Best Demo/UX", "Most Creative" — each with a trophy/medal icon. Under each category: scrollable grid of small project cards (thumbnail + title). Selected project has a glowing purple border and checkmark. User can select exactly 1 project per category. Own project is grayed out with "This is your project" label. Bottom: "Submit Votes" button (gradient, disabled until all 3 selected). Warning text: "You can only vote once."

## Screen 7: My Project (Submission Form)
Project submission page. Dark background. Stepper at top: "1. Basic Info → 2. Details → 3. Upload → 4. Review". Step 1 visible: Form with fields — "Project Name" (text input), "Create new project" or "Join existing project" toggle. If join: searchable dropdown of existing projects. If create: continue to step 2 with predefined questions as textareas — "What does your project do?" (max 280 chars with counter), "How did you get the idea?", "What was your journey?", "Tech stack used" (tag input). Step 3: Upload zone for video (drag & drop, "Max 60 seconds, landscape format"), upload zone for PDF (optional), upload zone for thumbnail screenshot. Step 4: Preview of everything before final submit. Big "Submit Project" button at the end. Note: "You cannot edit after submission."

## Screen 8: Admin Panel
Clean admin dashboard. Dark background. Left sidebar with nav: "Dashboard", "Projects", "Phases", "Results". Main content — Dashboard view: stats cards at top (Total Projects: 20, Participants: 48, Votes Cast: 35, Completion: 73%). Phase switcher: 4 pills/steps — "Submission", "Browsing", "Voting", "Results" — current phase highlighted in gradient. Below: recent activity log. Projects section would show a table with project name, team, status (submitted/pending), actions (edit/delete). Results section would show leaderboard per category with export to Excel button.
